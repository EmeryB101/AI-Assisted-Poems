Source Text with Erasure Markup

We were promised that the screens would bring us closer together, that every notification was a hand reaching across the void, but we have become experts at being alone together, sitting in the same room, eyes fixed on different worlds.

Touch has been replaced by the cold glass surface, where our fingers scroll endlessly through lives we'll never live, hungry for something we cannot name, feeding on light that never satisfies, leaving us more empty.

They said information would free us, but we drown in the endless stream of noise, forgetting how to listen, forgetting how to speak in voices that are truly our own, not echoes of algorithms.

Remember when we used to look up at the stars and wonder? Now we stare at artificial constellations, pixels arranged in patterns designed to hold us, captive in a universe of our own making.

But somewhere beneath the blue light, buried under layers of data and distance, we are still human, still capable of turning away, of remembering what it means to truly see.
